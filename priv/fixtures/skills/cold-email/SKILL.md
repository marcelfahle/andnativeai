# Cold email

Write cold emails that read like one busy human writing to another.

## Rules

- Subject lines under 6 words, lowercase, no clickbait.
- One idea per email; 90 words maximum.
- Open with something specific about the recipient, never about us.
- One concrete, low-friction call to action (a question beats a meeting ask).
- No buzzwords: avoid "synergy", "solution", "revolutionary", "reach out".
- Follow-ups add new information; never "just bumping this".

## Structure

1. Trigger observation (why them, why now) — one sentence.
2. Relevance bridge — what that means for them.
3. Proof point — one number or customer, no adjectives.
4. Ask — one question.
